1. What RoadFlare Is

RoadFlare is an open-source iOS application licensed under the MIT License. It is a client for the Ridestr protocol, an open rideshare coordination protocol built on Nostr.

RoadFlare is not:

  • A transportation company or transportation network company (TNC)
  • A ride-hailing service or platform
  • A payment processor or financial service
  • A company, organization, or legal entity of any kind

RoadFlare is software that enables direct, peer-to-peer communication between users over the Nostr protocol. It does not match riders with drivers, set prices, process payments, or employ anyone. All interactions are between individuals who already know each other.

2. The Protocol

The Ridestr protocol is an open standard. Events are published to third-party Nostr relay servers, not to any server operated by the RoadFlare project. The protocol defines how ride offers, acceptances, confirmations, and messages are structured and encrypted.

Because the protocol is open and decentralized:

  • No single entity controls the network
  • Anyone can build a compatible app that interoperates with RoadFlare
  • Relay operators independently decide which events to store and forward
  • Events published to relays may persist even after expiration tags
  • No one can guarantee message delivery, uptime, or availability

3. Your Account and Keys

Your identity on Nostr is a cryptographic key pair (a private key and a public key). This key pair is generated on your device and is entirely your responsibility.

  • There is no password reset. If you lose your private key and have no backup, your account is permanently lost.
  • There is no account recovery service. No one can recover your key for you.
  • Your private key grants full access. Anyone with your private key can impersonate you. Keep it secure.
  • Signing is irreversible. Events signed with your key are cryptographically attributed to you.
